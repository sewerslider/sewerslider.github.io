Tracking in school

Late december this year, a fleet of devices used for attendence checking was installed at my school. The gist of it is that now students will have to either put their faces in front of the camera or use a card provided by the school to show that they come to school that day.

Ultimately speaking, as a privacy-consious person, I don’t like this device. To use this device, They need to have at least a picture of my face. Since my school started installing a fleet of this device without even considering the privacy implications, I have to give them a picture of my face. This is made worse by the fact that they don’t disclose any information about it either - the most I have heard about it is their announcement back in sometime around late November. This means that I don’t know the servers they connect it to, what information they have about me, etc. Sure, the devices might not connect to any server and the people operating the machine might not request much of my personal info but I don’t know because I can’t verify it.

It doesn’t stop at that though. For some reason, even though they have a fleet of devices with questionable privacy that every students must use just to check attendence, every class still have to do the same thing to report back to the teacher if they request it. So I guess our privacy is being invaded for … no particular reason now apparently.
